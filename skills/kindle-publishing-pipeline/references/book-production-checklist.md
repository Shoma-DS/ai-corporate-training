# Book Production Checklist

## Strategy

- Reader is specific enough to write for one job, problem, or aspiration.
- Promise is concrete and not exaggerated.
- Book has a reason to exist beyond blog-post length.
- Competing titles and reader reviews were checked or marked for research.
- Claims that need sources are marked.

## Outline

- Table of contents follows a useful progression.
- Each chapter has a reader outcome.
- Repeated chapters are not just topic summaries.
- Examples fit the target reader's work or life context.
- The book includes templates, checklists, workflows, or decision rules where useful.

## Manuscript

- Front matter exists: title page, author name, copyright note if needed.
- Chapters use consistent heading levels.
- No manual tab indents.
- Links and footnotes are intentional and checked.
- Images have usage rights and alt/description notes where relevant.
- No private data, client secrets, personal data, or copied paid content.
- AI usage notes are recorded for disclosure review.

## Metadata

- Title/subtitle match cover and manuscript.
- Description sells the outcome without making false guarantees.
- Keywords are reader/search oriented, not spam.
- Categories fit the book and are verified near upload.
- Author/contributor names are consistent.
- Rights and territories are consciously chosen.
- KDP Select decision is explicit.

## Cover

- Title readable at thumbnail size.
- Subtitle supports the promise without overcrowding.
- Genre/category signal is clear.
- Assets are licensed, original, public domain, or properly sourced.
- Ebook cover dimensions/file size pass validation.
- Paperback cover is treated separately from ebook cover.

## Submission

- KDP official requirements checked near final upload.
- Manuscript previewed in Kindle Previewer or KDP preview.
- Validation report has no critical issues.
- Manual upload checklist is updated.
- Account, tax, bank, royalty, and pricing data are kept outside public repo.

## Launch

- Store description is final.
- Author bio is ready.
- Launch copy is prepared for email, SNS, website, or Amazon author page.
- Review request language follows platform rules.
- Post-publication correction workflow is noted.
